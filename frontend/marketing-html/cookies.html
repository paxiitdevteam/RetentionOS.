Cookie Policy

Last Updated: January 2025

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

2. How We Use Cookies

RetentionOS uses cookies to enhance your experience, analyze site usage, and assist in our marketing efforts. We use both session cookies (temporary) and persistent cookies (remain on your device).

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as security, network management, and accessibility.

  • Authentication cookies: Remember your login status
  • Security cookies: Protect against fraud and abuse
  • Load balancing cookies: Distribute traffic across servers

3.2 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously.

  • Page views and navigation patterns
  • Time spent on pages
  • Error messages and performance issues

3.3 Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization, such as remembering your preferences.

  • Language preferences
  • Region settings
  • User interface preferences

3.4 Marketing Cookies

These cookies are used to track visitors across websites to display relevant advertisements.

  • Advertising effectiveness
  • Campaign performance
  • Retargeting efforts

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that set cookies on your device. These include:

  • Google Analytics: Website analytics
  • Stripe: Payment processing
  • Customer support tools: Help desk functionality

These third parties have their own privacy policies and cookie practices.

5. Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies in various ways. Please keep in mind that removing or blocking cookies can impact your user experience.

Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to:

  • See what cookies you have and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block cookies from specific sites
  • Block all cookies
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

Browser-Specific Instructions

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies
  • Firefox: Options → Privacy & Security → Cookies
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Cookies
  • Edge: Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Cookies

6. Cookie Consent

When you first visit our website, we may ask for your consent to use non-essential cookies. You can withdraw your consent at any time by adjusting your browser settings or contacting us.

7. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature. We currently do not respond to DNT signals, but we respect your privacy choices through cookie management.
